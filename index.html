About Our Team

From a classroom project to a competitive robotics team

Our Journey

Founded in 2022 at Amplus Academy's Durango Campus in Las Vegas, Nevada, the Archerbots started as a simple robotics class. What began as a curiosity-driven experiment quickly evolved into a passionate team of student engineers.

Despite limited resources and funding challenges, we've consistently built competitive robots and made our mark in the FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC) community. Our journey reflects the resilience and determination of our student members.

Competing in the 2025-2026 "Decode" season, we've achieved our most competitive robot yet, thanks to a significant influx of new materials and resources. However, our potential remains untapped due to travel limitations that prevented us from advancing further in competition.

Murphy's Law

Internally, we call ourselves "Murphy's Law" - a nod to our experience that anything that can go wrong, will go wrong. But we've learned to embrace these challenges as opportunities for growth. Every obstacle we overcome makes us stronger engineers and better teammates.

Decode Season Robot

For the 2025-2026 Decode season, our team designed a robot focused on versatility and precision. With a custom intake mechanism for handling organic samples and a sophisticated scoring system, our robot demonstrates our commitment to both technical excellence and strategic gameplay.

The design philosophy centered on modularity, allowing for quick adjustments between matches. Our programming team implemented advanced sensor fusion for autonomous navigation, while our build team created a robust chassis capable of handling the demanding game elements.

Engineering Portfolio

Documenting our journey and engineering process

What is an Engineering Portfolio?

An Engineering Portfolio is a comprehensive document that tells the story of our team's season, showcasing our engineering process, design decisions, and problem-solving approaches. It's a critical component of FTC judging, demonstrating how we think, plan, and execute our robot development.

The portfolio includes detailed sections on our engineering methodology, CAD designs, mathematical calculations, design iterations, and reflections on our successes and challenges. Judges use this document to evaluate our technical skills, creativity, and engineering mindset.

2025-2026 Decode Season Portfolio

Our 2025-2026 season portfolio documents our journey through the "Decode" challenge. It features our initial brainstorming sessions, CAD models of our robot iterations, technical calculations for our mechanisms, and our strategic approach to game elements.

The portfolio highlights our design process, our autonomous routines, and our team's reflections on competition performance and areas for improvement. This document was instrumental in our judging presentations and helped showcase our technical capabilities to judges at regional competitions.

Outreach & Community

Engaging our community with creativity and passion

The Alligator Adventure

One of our proudest achievements extends beyond the competition field. For our school's homecoming dance, we designed and built a full-scale alligator costume from scratch. This wasn't just a costume - it was a functioning prop with articulated jaws that brought our school to life.

The alligator made appearances at school assemblies and community events, showcasing our team's creativity and engineering skills to a broader audience. The project required skills in mechanical engineering, electronics, and performance - demonstrating that robotics is about more than just building competition robots.

This project perfectly embodies our team spirit and commitment to making STEM exciting and accessible to everyone in our community.

How to Donate

Supporting our team is easy! Follow these simple steps:

  1. Click the link below to open our school's donation form.
  2. Enter the donation amount you wish to contribute.
  3. Under "Sport / Activity / Club," select or enter Robotics so your donation is directed to our team.
  4. In the notes/comments field, write "Archerbots Robotics Team #22818" so your donation is properly credited.
  5. Complete your payment information and submit the form.
  6. You will receive a confirmation email and tax receipt for your donation.

All donations go directly toward supporting our robotics program, including travel expenses, materials, and competition fees.
